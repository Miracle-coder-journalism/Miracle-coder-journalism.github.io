Nanjing: City of Art

There’s art to be found around every corner of Nanjing, followed by countless breathtaking, and heart-stopping moments. Touring around, you are embracing a city of beauty, weaved together by passion, novelty, and a strong sense of mission. 

Whenever I go to a city, I love to visit the exhibitions and theaters there, especially the works composed by local artists and students because they somewhat reflect the culture, history and spirit of the city.

Nanjing boasts rich culture and genius artists. It is the passion and imagination exhibited in artistic designs that represent the human spirit and novelty of this city.

What I’m going to share next are art works composed in Nanjing, either by artists or by talented students. I'd love to call them "moveable feasts".

In my story, various artistic designs that left me the deepest impression will be weaved together to form a bigger picture, including paintings, engravings, musical plays and graphic designs. Each has its own story. Despite their difference in forms, I find it hard to set them apart in that I’m able to sense the similarity between them. Moreover, they will surely be attractive to foreign tourists who are art lovers.

The Play Hai Zi

The art story of Nanjing starts with passion. The play here on stage is named Hai Zi, in English meaning ‘children’. Two years ago, talented students in Nanjing University put their heads together to cast this play in memory of the poet Hai Zi, whose name embodies the meaning of ‘son of the ocean’ and bears the same pronunciation with ‘children’ in Chinese. Despite the fact that Hai Zi died at a young age, his poems are bursting with vitality to inspire countless adolescents. Therefore, the genius students named the play in the Chinese expression of ‘children’ in order to accentuate the robust vitality of the spirit of the poet Hai Zi.

The cast group devoted countless efforts to the preparation. When the play was on show, I was deeply touched by their preoccupied performance. The theater in Nanjing University was rather simple, with no more than a few walls. Nevertheless, the group spared no effort to decorate the stage and convey true emotions through every single gesture during the performance. Some students took on more than one tasks in the performance, at one point playing the piano and the next minute appearing on stage, fully absorbed in the narration to reveal the seeking journey of poet Hai Zi.

The play Hai Zi is a mirror of countless passionate artworks in Nanjing. Through passion, artists burn their flames of art on and on forever.

Not only is the play Hai Zi full of passion, but it’s also brimming with creativity, which constantly strikes you with refreshment while offering you food for thought. If you are seeking for something novel in life, Nanjing will never let you down!

Have you ever read a monthly magazine of only one page? Have you ever thought of how classic movies would turn out if human roles were substituted by cats? Have you ever imagined that a bland-looking book actually boasts a 3-D amusement park made out of pages for you? Have you ever pictured that rabbit and dog can bond together to form a whole new species called ‘rabbit dog’ under an artist’s magic paintbrush?

If you haven’t experienced such marvels before, don’t hesitate to come to Nanjing! A random visit to any art gallery will open up a brand new world for you! Not only can novelty been seen within the overall design of artworks, but they can also be captured even in the detailed inscriptions hidden in the corner of the canvas.

Qin Qi's Observation into an Orphanage

The engravings composed by Qinqi are named ‘Wandering in the Dark’. They mark his observation into an orphanage, where children are struggling both physically and mentally with tough living conditions and the absence of psychological care.

Literally, these orphans are ‘wondering in the dark’ since their physical and mental state can hardly been seen by the public because of their separate living treks. Qin Qi observed flaws in social support systems and the absence of public attention for the underprivileged groups. In an attempt to bring these children to the spotlight, Qin Qi picked up his paintbrush and produced the engravings that helped orphans call out for social support.

To me, Qin Qi’s compositions carry with it spectacular power to heal the world. If you take a deeper dive into the artwork of Nanjing, you will constantly meet with such forces that sometimes even move you to tears.
